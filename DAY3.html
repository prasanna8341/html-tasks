Jump to Mutton Biryani Recipe



Mutton Biryani, a flavorful and aromatic dish, is a popular South Asian delight. Tender mutton pieces cooked with fragrant spices and layered with fluffy basmati rice, make for a mouthwatering treat. This rich and savory meal is perfect for special occasions and gatherings.


Tips:

Tips to cook mutton biriyani:

  • Use fresh and tender mutton: Choose fresh mutton pieces with a good amount of fat for flavor and tenderness
  • Marinate mutton properly: Marinate mutton in a mixture of yogurt, spices, and herbs for at least 30 minutes to tenderize and flavor the meat.
  • Use aromatic spices: Use whole spices like cinnamon, cardamom, and cloves to add depth and aroma to the biryani.
  • Soak rice correctly: Soak basmati rice in water for 30 minutes to an hour to make it fluffy and separate.
  • Layer biryani correctly: Layer mutton, rice, and spices in a specific order to ensure even cooking and flavor distribution.
  • Cook on low heat: Cook biryani on low heat for 10-15 minutes to allow flavors to meld and rice to cook evenly.

    Mutton Biryani Recipe

      Step 1: Prepare the Ingredients

    1. 1 kg mutton pieces
    2. 2 cups basmati rice
    3. 1 onion, chopped
    4. 2 cloves garlic, minced
    5. 1 inch ginger, grated
    6. 1 tsp cumin powder
    7. 1 tsp coriander powder
    8. 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
    9. 1/2 tsp red chili powder
    10. Salt, to taste
    11. 2 tbsp oil
    12. 2 cups water
    Step 2: Cook the Mutton
            
      
            
            
    • Heat oil in a pan over medium heat.
      • 
            
    • Add chopped onion and cook until soft.
      • 
            
    • Add minced garlic, grated ginger, cumin powder, coriander powder, turmeric powder, and red chili powder. Cook for 1 minute.
      • 
            
    • Add mutton pieces and cook until tender.
      • 
            
    • Add salt to taste.
            
    
           
     Step 3: Cook the Rice
    

    
       
    • Rinse basmati rice and soak for 30 minutes.
      • 
    
    •  Drain water and cook rice in 2 cups of water until al dente.
      • 
    
    •  Drain excess water and set aside.
Step 4: Assemble the Biryani
    
    

 
  • In a large pot, create a layer of rice.
    • 
 
  •  Add a layer of cooked mutton on top of the rice.
    • 
 
  • Repeat layers until all ingredients are used.
    • 
 
  •  Cover with a lid and cook on low heat for 10 minutes.
Step 5:Serve and Enjoy!

- Serve hot, garnished with fresh cilantro and a dollop of raita (yogurt sauce) if desired.
